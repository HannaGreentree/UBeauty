about us

U Beauty is a team of Ukrainian professionals who moved to the UK about three years ago. Many of us owned beauty salons in our home country, but for well-known reasons, we had to leave and start again here in the UK. Thanks to the kindness of the British people, this became possible. Today, we represent local beauty and cleaning businesses, serving British customers with the aim to deliver service “better than high street.”

While we are proud of our Ukrainian heritage, we also understand what British clients value and we prioritise it. We are trustworthy professionals with qualifications that meet UK standards. We are friendly, clean, and hold hygiene certificates. We use only disposable tools or fully sterilised equipment for every customer. We value nature, appreciate a natural look, and always ensure excellent value for money.

We are suitable for OCD and autistic clients, because we know how important perfection and consistency are to you. Your safety and satisfaction are our top priorities. Our goal is to make you feel beautiful, relaxed, and confident, so you can enjoy life to the fullest.

why us

Since starting our business in the UK, we have been committed to using the latest technology, materials, and products. We have refreshed our knowledge, upgraded our techniques, and made sure we can provide the best possible service. We are always ready to go the extra mile — even adjusting our schedules to make things more convenient for our clients. And while quality is our priority, our service is always excellent value for money.

If you’re still reading, here’s a slightly longer — and a bit funny — version of “Why Us.” In Ukraine, especially during Soviet times, girls often grew up with strong expectations about being tidy, domestic, and “marriage-ready.”

You have no idea how many times our mothers and grandmothers told us: “Clean your room or no one will marry you.” “No one will marry you if you can’t cook.” “A girl must always look neat, or she’ll stay alone.” “Keep your nails clean and filed, or no one will marry you.” Naturally, wanting to get married one day, we learned all the domestic skills we could!

Another fun fact: when we were kids during Soviet times, private services like nail salons, hairdressers, and brow bars were rare. So, we learned to do manicures, pedicures, brows, semi-permanent lashes, and even haircuts — all by ourselves.

There were always someone in the family who cut hair to the rest of the family. Just saying, but we also learned how to fix clothes and to repair some appliances.
